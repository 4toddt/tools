How it works. Each fiscal year ends on the last Saturday of April. Fiscal weeks run Sunday through Saturday. Each quarter is 13 weeks, split as 5+4+4 (the first month of every quarter gets the extra week). Fiscal months align to weeks, not to calendar months, so they almost always start a few days before or after the 1st.

Why some years have 53 weeks. A normal fiscal year is 52 weeks (364 days), one or two days short of a calendar year, so the year-end Saturday drifts earlier in April each year. Once it would drift before April 24 (the earliest a "last Saturday of April" can fall in a 30-day month), the rule snaps forward to April 29 or 30, adding a 53rd week. The extra week is appended to fiscal month 12 (April), making Q4 a 5-4-5 instead of 5-4-4. Equivalently: 53-week years occur whenever April has 5 Saturdays and the prior year-end was on the 4th Saturday.
